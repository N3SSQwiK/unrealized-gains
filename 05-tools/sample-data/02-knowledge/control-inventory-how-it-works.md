## What It Is
The control inventory is a centralized register of all risk controls across wealth management. Each control maps to a risk category, has an owner, and is tested on a defined cycle.

## Structure
- **Control ID**: Unique identifier (WM-CTRL-XXXX)
- **Risk Category**: Operational, market, credit, compliance, technology
- **Owner**: Team or individual responsible
- **Testing Cycle**: Annual, semi-annual, quarterly, or continuous
- **Last Tested**: Date of most recent effectiveness test
- **Status**: Effective, partially effective, ineffective, not yet tested

## How Testing Works
1. Control owner documents the control procedure
2. Testing team executes the test plan
3. Results are graded: effective / partially effective / ineffective
4. Findings are logged and remediation tracked
5. Exceptions escalate to the risk committee

## Common Questions
**"Why do we test controls we know work?"** — Regulatory requirement. Effectiveness must be independently verified on a defined cycle.

**"Who decides the testing cycle?"** — Risk committee, based on the control's risk rating and regulatory guidance.

**"What happens when a control fails?"** — A finding is logged, a remediation plan is required within 30 days, and the control is re-tested within 90 days.
